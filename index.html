01. Motivation & Research

1.1 The Why: Choosing the Active Track

Our team chose the Active Track because we recognized a fundamental gap in how fitness is approached within university environments like XJTLU. While students are health-conscious, the "Active" lifestyle remains unoptimized due to data opacity. We chose this track to leverage software engineering to provide real-time, actionable insights. By focusing on Active technology, we address "gym anxiety" and "training plateaus" through a scientific feedback loop—using MET calculations and real-time occupancy tracking—to empower students to move from intuitive training to precise, high-performance habits.

1.2 The Gap: Academic & Commercial Analysis

Paper 01: Lyons et al. [1]

  • ✔ Proved that self-monitoring is the most effective technique for exercise adherence.
  • ✔ Validated electronic lifestyle activity monitors for behavioral change.
  • ✔ Provided framework for standardized exercise data tracking.
  • ✖ Ignored the high cognitive load fitness beginners face during goal setting.
  • ✖ Failed to explore intuitive visual-based goal setting to lower barriers.
  • ✖ Missed the link between personal data and physical environment.

Paper 02: Haynes [2]

  • ✔ Identified how prolonged decision time negatively impacts satisfaction.
  • ✔ Established the importance of minimizing cognitive effort in selection.
  • ✔ Tested boundaries of choice overload in consumer decision making.
  • ✖ Failed to account for spatial constraints in exercise delivery models.
  • ✖ Missed the impact of real-time gym load on the decision-making loop.
  • ✖ Ignored flexible intermediate solutions like "at-home private training."

Paper 03: Zayim et al. [3]

  • ✔ Estimated cognitive load in mobile health record applications.
  • ✔ Defined data intimidation (BMI/Caloric burn) as a barrier.
  • ✔ Used cognitive task analysis to evaluate UI efficiency.
  • ✖ Failed to integrate live spatial data into the health record loop.
  • ✖ Lacked empathy for novice users overwhelmed by professional stats.
  • ✖ Missed opportunities to break spatial constraints via rigid digital models.

Paper 04: Cselik et al. [4]

  • ✔ Proved the physical environment significantly impacts motivation.
  • ✔ Provided public health perspectives on fitness center deterministic factors.
  • ✔ Identified gym atmosphere as a key for long-term exercise behavior.
  • ✖ Lacked a software-based fix for unpredictable gym "blind boxes."
  • ✖ Failed to provide real-time occupancy feedback to mitigate social anxiety.
  • ✖ Missed the integration of "at-home" flexibility to break rigid delivery.

Product: Keep

  • ✔ Robust Content: Features professional, standardized exercise video libraries.
  • ✔ Reduced Decision Time: Established a seamless online content consumption loop.
  • ✔ Flexible Models: Adopted subscription models that alleviate financial burden.
  • ✖ High Cognitive Load: Forces users to confront intimidating BMI/Caloric numbers.
  • ✖ Rigid Delivery: Limits users to solitary online workouts with no hybrid flexibility.
  • ✖ Missing Context: Fails to provide real-time occupancy data for offline facilities.

Product: Leke Gym

  • ✔ Financial Flexibility: Abandoned hard-selling for smart pay-as-you-go chain models.
  • ✔ Low Friction: Seamless "booking-payment-offline check-in" loop.
  • ✔ Automation: Effectively used smart hardware to reduce manual check-in effort.
  • ✖ Blind Box Experience: Offline status remains unpredictable without live data.
  • ✖ Social Anxiety: Leaves users vulnerable to equipment unavailability and crowds.
  • ✖ Rigid Spatiality: Limits service to designated offline physical stores only.

Product: Xunji

  • ✔ Hardcore Tracking: Accurately records workout trajectories and weight progression.
  • ✔ Self-Monitoring: Implements effective behavior change through quantified-self metrics.
  • ✔ Scientific Focus: Professional focus on lifting trajectories and specific output.
  • ✖ Cognitive Overload: Exposure to complex data intimidates fitness beginners.
  • ✖ Lack of Empathy: Missed visual-based goal setting to lower entry barriers.
  • ✖ No Live Dynamics: Lacks integration with real-time spatial occupancy data.

Product: Supermonkey

  • ✔ Minimal Decision: O2O boutique loop minimizes cognitive effort in booking.
  • ✔ UX Design: Immersive offline class environments improve initial motivation.
  • ✔ Pay-per-class: Abandoned aggressive traditional gym financial strategies.
  • ✖ Congestion Gaps: Fails to mitigate unpredictable equipment and space congestion.
  • ✖ Rigid Service: Limits users to solitary classes or solitary gym visits.
  • ✖ Spatial Constraint: No flexible "at-home private coaching" for busy users.
